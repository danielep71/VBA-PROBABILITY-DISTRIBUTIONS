# Survival tail relative-accuracy boundary

The standard-normal survival core (shared by `Normal_Survival`, `Lognormal_Survival`, `NormalStandard_Survival`) keeps ~1E-17 ABSOLUTE accuracy in the tail but loses RELATIVE accuracy as z grows. This locates where the tight central contracts stop holding, so their domains are restricted from measurement, not guessed. Regenerated by exporting `survival_boundary_grid.csv`.

| 3.75 | 8.842e-05 | 2.25e-13 |
| 4.00 | 3.167e-05 | 1.24e-13 |
| 4.50 | 3.398e-06 | 6.27e-12 |

> 5E-15 holds through z=2.75 (worst 3.63E-15) and breaches at z=3.0; 2E-14 holds through z=3.25 and breaches at z=3.5. Hence: `Normal_Survival` / `Lognormal_Survival` tight (5E-15) domain restricted to |z| <= 2.75; `NormalStandard_Survival` (2E-14) to |z| <= 3.25. Deeper-tail relative behavior recorded as `SurvivalTailRel` in numerical_limitations.csv. Even at z=6 the survival value is right to ~5E-10 relative, far better than 1-CDF cancellation.
